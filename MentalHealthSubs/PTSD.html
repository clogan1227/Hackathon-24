PTSD

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is like carrying a heavy backpack filled with memories and emotions from a traumatic experience. It's as if your mind is stuck in the past, replaying the event over and over again, even when you try to move forward. Just as anxiety can make you feel on edge and hyperaware of your surroundings, PTSD can make you feel constantly on guard, as if danger is always lurking around the corner. It can also cause nightmares and flashbacks, making you feel like you're reliving the trauma. PTSD can make it hard to trust others and can lead to feelings of isolation and disconnection from the world. Like anxiety, PTSD is a natural response to trauma, and it's important to seek support and treatment to help process the experience and find healing.
